I am
An Artist. A Photographer. A Student. Mckenzie Hamilton.

I work mainly with photography and oil paints but regularly experiment with fabrics/fashion; photography is one of my favourite activities. Unlike oils, which take longer to start up, it's fun from beginning to end. Oils/painting, in general, is how I began being interested in art, so it’s something I've always done – it is comfortable. Photography/painting is the easiest way to express important themes/topics to me, for example, identity, acceptance, and spirituality* (mainly with my paintings)

I also enjoy the digital editing process. I find inspiration randomly, everything has creative potential in my eyes e.g. A blank slab of wood with endless possibilities or even a scene I can tell will be a nice image and then I develop the idea with plans, mind maps or mood boards. Things that inspire me include music, nature, dance – anything creative and natural. Also, I read novels/historical texts because there’s so much creativity there, especially in things I would never think about/ words I would never use.

Although I do a lot of different things, they all relate to make up my practice as I can use them to express any ideas on my mind, which I enjoy. Some things that are a constant theme in my work is culture, The culture that I'm in—being young and black in the UK—and the Caribbean, African, and Asian influences in my life from people I know or beliefs that I've always known, etc. Im influenced heavily by the people around me.
